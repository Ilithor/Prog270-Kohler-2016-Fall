Black Hole Farming

According to what we know now, black holes output energy depending on the mass of said black hole. The more mass it has, the less energy it outputs, but it outputs more energy overtime. This will not be possible until we further understand how Hawking Radiation can be harnessed as an energy source.

The table below represents an approximate energy output and life length with a black hole with a mass of 1 as a base comparison.

Black Hole Mass Energy Output (*) Life Length
2 1/2^2 = 1/4 2^3 = 8
100 1/100^2 = 0.0001 100^3 = 1,000,000
.5 1/.5^2 = 4 .5^3 = 0.125

(*) of Total Power
